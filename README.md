# Hobby vs. Business: A Tax Decision Tree

## Overview

Many people earn income from activities they enjoy — such as crafts, tutoring, consulting, online selling, or other side projects.

A common tax question is:

> **"Is this activity a hobby, or is it a business?"**

The answer is not based only on how much money is earned. Tax classification depends on the overall facts and circumstances, including the taxpayer's intent, how the activity is conducted, and whether there is a genuine effort to operate for profit.

This project converts that tax concept into a simple decision tree to demonstrate how complex tax rules can be organized into practical decision paths.

## Key Considerations

### Indicators of a Business Activity

An activity may be considered a business when the taxpayer:

- Operates in a businesslike manner
- Maintains complete and accurate records
- Tracks income and expenses
- Advertises or markets services/products
- Makes efforts to improve profitability
- Has a reasonable expectation of earning a profit

Examples:

- Freelance services
- Consulting work
- Tutoring services
- Online sales
- Professional services

---

### Indicators of a Hobby Activity

An activity may be considered a hobby when it is primarily pursued for:

- Personal enjoyment
- Recreation
- Artistic expression
- Personal interest rather than profit

Examples:

- Making crafts occasionally without a profit objective
- Selling items mainly to share a personal interest
- Activities where profit is not the primary goal

## Example Scenario

### College Student Nail Business

A college student provides nail services to friends and family.

Facts:

- Charges $20–$35 per appointment
- Purchases supplies
- Accepts payments
- Provides services regularly
- May advertise through social media

Questions to consider:

1. Is there a genuine intention to make a profit?
2. Is the activity operated in a businesslike way?
3. Are income and expenses tracked?
4. Is this an occasional activity or an ongoing operation?

The answer depends on the complete picture.
